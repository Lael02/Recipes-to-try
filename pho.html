Pho

4 servings | Time: 3h+

Ingredients

  • Broth:
    1. Beef knuckles or leg bones, 5/6 punds
    2. Water, 5 liters
    3. 2 onions, cut
    4. fresh ginger, medium piece
    5. 2 cinnamon sticks
    6. 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
    7. 1 tablespoon fennel seeds
    8. 6 whole star anise
    9. 6 whole cloves
    10. 1 black cardamom pod
    11. 1 ½ tablespoons sea salt
    12. 1/4 cup fish sauce
    13. 1-inch piece of yellow rock sugar

  • 1 pound dried/fresh “banh pho” noodles
  • 230g raw eye of round, sirloin, or tri-tip steak, thinly sliced
  • Small onion, thinly sliced
  • Cilantro, chopped
  • Optional: fresh mint, Thai basil, bean sprouts, lime wedges, sliced green or red chilies,
    fish sauce, hoisin sauce, sriracha/ chili sauce

Instructions

  1. Place beef bones in a stockpot and cover with cold water.
  2. Bring to a boil over high heat for 3 to 5 minutes. Remove cum and foam will float to the top.
  3. Drain the bones, rinse with warm water, and scrub the stockpot clean,
    and then return bones to the pot and add 6 quarts of cold water.
  4. Preheat oven broiler to high and line a baking sheet with foil.
    Place onion quarters and halved ginger on the baking sheet and broil for 10 to 15 minutes until nicely charred.
  5. Place spices in a dry frying pan over low heat and toast for about 5 minutes until fragrant.
  6. Transfer the spices to a muslin bag or cheesecloth and seal with twine.
  7. Bring the stockpot to a boil and reduce the heat.
  8. Add the charred onions, ginger, spice bag, salt, fish sauce, and rock sugar. Gently simmer uncovered for 3 hours, skimming off any foam that rises to the surface.
  9. Remove bones, onions, and ginger and strain the broth.
  10. Place dried pho noodles in a bowl and cover with hot water. (Soak for 15 to 20 minutes or until they are soft and opaque).
  11. If the noodles are fresh, rinse with cold water.
  12. Bring a medium saucepan of water to a boil. Add noodles and cook briefly (about 10 seconds) until they soften.
  13. Drain the noodles and divide them evenly between serving bowls,
  14. Arrange slices of raw meat over the noodles in each bowl and carefully ladle the very hot broth over the meat (cooking it).
  15. Top with onion slices and cilantro and serve with your favorite pho toppings.
